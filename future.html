Research Papers Feed

Landmark and recent papers shaping the future of AI and computer science.

LLM
Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani, Shazeer, Parmar et al. — Google Brain
NeurIPS 2017
Introduced the Transformer architecture — the foundation upon which every modern large language model is built. Eliminated recurrence in favor of pure attention mechanisms, enabling massive parallelization.
2017 · 100K+ citationsarXiv
LLM
GPT-4 Technical Report
OpenAI Research Team
OpenAI Technical Report 2023
Introduces GPT-4, a multimodal large language model achieving human-level performance on numerous professional and academic benchmarks, including the bar exam and SAT.
2023 · GPT-4arXiv
SCIENCE AI
Highly Accurate Protein Structure Prediction with AlphaFold
Jumper, Evans, Pritzel et al. — DeepMind
Nature, 2021
AlphaFold2 solved one of biology's greatest challenges — predicting 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences. Has since predicted structures of virtually all known proteins, transforming drug discovery.
2021 · Nobel-worthyNature
REINFORCEMENT LEARNING
Mastering the Game of Go with Deep Neural Networks and Tree Search
Silver, Huang, Maddison et al. — DeepMind
Nature, 2016
AlphaGo became the first AI to defeat a world champion Go player. Demonstrated deep reinforcement learning combined with Monte Carlo tree search could conquer tasks thought impossible for machines.
2016 · AlphaGoNature
REASONING
DeepSeek-R1: Incentivizing Reasoning Capability in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning
DeepSeek-AI Research Team
arXiv, 2025
Presents a pure RL approach to developing reasoning in LLMs without supervised fine-tuning data. DeepSeek-R1 matches OpenAI's o1 on math and coding benchmarks at a fraction of the training cost.
2025 · DeepSeek R1arXiv
VISION
An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: Transformers for Image Recognition at Scale
Dosovitskiy, Beyer, Kolesnikov et al. — Google Brain
ICLR 2021
Vision Transformer (ViT) applied the Transformer architecture directly to image patches, challenging the dominance of CNNs. Foundation for all modern multimodal AI and computer vision systems.
2021 · ViTarXiv
ALIGNMENT
Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback
Bai, Jones, Ndousse et al. — Anthropic
arXiv, 2022
Introduces a training approach where AI models critique and revise their own outputs according to a set of principles — enabling scalable AI safety without large amounts of human feedback.
2022 · Claude FoundationarXiv
ROBOTICS
RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models Transfer Web Knowledge to Robotic Control
Brohan, Brown, Carbajal et al. — Google DeepMind
CoRL 2023
Shows that visual-language models trained on web data can be directly applied to robotic control, enabling robots to reason about novel tasks from text descriptions and visual observations.
2023 · Physical AIarXiv
QUANTUM
Quantum Supremacy Using a Programmable Superconducting Processor
Arute, Arya, Babbush et al. — Google Quantum AI
Nature, 2019
Google's Sycamore processor performed a specific computation in 200 seconds that would take the world's best supercomputer 10,000 years — the first experimental demonstration of quantum supremacy.
2019 · Quantum LeapNature

Future Technologies

Transformative technologies that will define the next 50 years of human civilization.

⚛️
Quantum Computing
ETA: 2025–2035 · IBM, Google, IonQ

Harnesses superposition and entanglement for computations impossible classically. Will revolutionize cryptography, drug discovery, materials science, and financial optimization.

QubitsSuperpositionError CorrectionPost-Quantum

Artificial General Intelligence
ETA: 2027–2040 · OpenAI, DeepMind

AI systems capable of any intellectual task a human can perform. The transition from narrow AI to AGI represents the most transformative event in human technological history.

World ModelsTransfer LearningSelf-Improvement

DNA & Biological Computing
ETA: 2030–2050 · Microsoft

Computing using DNA strands to store and process information. A single gram of DNA stores 215 petabytes of data, operating at room temperature with extraordinary energy efficiency.

DNA StorageSynthetic BiologyMolecular Computing

Photonic Computing
ETA: 2026–2035 · Lightmatter, Intel

Using photons (light) instead of electrons. Offers massive speed improvements, near-zero heat, and perfect for AI inference. Lightmatter's Passage chip processes AI at the speed of light.

Optical AISilicon PhotonicsLow Latency

Neuromorphic Computing
ETA: 2025–2030 · Intel Loihi 3

Brain-inspired processors mimicking neural architecture. Spiking neural networks use 1000× less power than GPUs — perfect for always-on edge AI in everything from hearing aids to satellites.

Spiking NNsEdge AILow Power

Brain-Computer Interfaces
ETA: 2025–2040 · Neuralink, Synchron

Direct neural-digital interfaces enabling bidirectional brain-computer communication. From restoring paralysis to cognitive enhancement, thought-to-text, and neural internet access.

Neural RecordingNeuroprostheticsThought Input

Foundation models bringing general-purpose reasoning to physical AI. Robots that learn from observation, adapt to novel environments, and work alongside humans in every industry.

Embodied AIDexterous HandsPhysical AI
